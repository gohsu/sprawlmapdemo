SNDI

Kolkata

City in West Bengal, India

The cumulative and incremental level of street network disconnectivity in Kolkata do not follow the same trend. Cumulative SNDi is increasing steadily over time.

Taking into account all roads in Kolkata, the overall level of street network sprawl is 4.1. In Kolkata, new street layouts initially increased in level of sprawl, until the peak in 1991-2000.

The total level of sprawl in Kolkata is lower than the regional level. Kolkata does not follow the regional trend: Between 1991-2000 and 2001-2014, regional SNDi was increasing instead.

Compared to India, the level of sprawl in Kolkata is higher overall. Kolkata does not follow the national trend. Between 1991-2000 and 2001-2014, national SNDi was increasing instead.

To date, Kolkata is the 13th-most disconnected city out of the 130 cities in West Bengal. It has fallen in the ranks since 1975. In <1975, it ranked 5th, ranked 6th in 1976-1990 and ranked 5th in 1991-2000 and ranked 10th in 2001-2014.

SNDI

Toronto

City in Ontario, Canada

The cumulative and incremental level of street network disconnectivity in Toronto do not follow the same trend. Cumulative SNDi is increasing initially, but starts decreasing after 1991-2000.

Taking into account all roads in Toronto, the overall level of street network sprawl is 2.21. In Toronto, new street layouts initially increased in level of sprawl, until the peak in 1976-1990.

The total level of sprawl in Toronto is lower than the regional level. Toronto does not follow the regional trend: Between 1976-1990 and 1991-2000, regional SNDi was increasing instead.

Compared to Canada, the level of sprawl in Toronto is lower overall. Toronto does not follow the national trend. Between 1976-1990 and 1991-2000, national SNDi was increasing instead.

To date, Toronto is the 7th-most disconnected city out of the 21 cities in Ontario. It has fallen in the ranks since 1975. In <1975, it ranked 8th, ranked 13th in 1976-1990 and ranked 16th in 1991-2000 and ranked 14th in 2001-2014.
